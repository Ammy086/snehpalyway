❓ Common Questions About Autism Therapy

Can speech therapy help children with non-speaking autism?

Absolutely! Non-speaking children communicate in many ways. We help them through AAC devices, visual supports, sign language, and other alternative communication methods. Every child deserves a voice!

How is autism speech therapy different from regular speech therapy?

Autism therapy focuses on social communication, pragmatic language, and sensory needs alongside speech clarity. We work on how language is used in social situations, not just pronunciation.

Will therapy help my child "fit in" with other children?

Our goal isn't to change your child—it's to help them communicate confidently and build meaningful relationships in their own way. We celebrate neurodiversity and build on their unique strengths!

How do you handle sensory sensitivities during therapy?

Every session is customized. If your child is sensitive to noise or touch, we adjust the environment and activities. We have quiet spaces, fidget toys, and flexible timings.

Can therapy help with behaviors like repetitive speech or echolalia?

Yes! We work on functional communication and social skills. With patience and the right techniques, we can help redirect repetitive patterns toward more interactive communication.

Common Questions

Everything parents want to know.

What age children do you work with?

We work with children from 2.5 to 8 years old. Whether your child is just starting their learning journey or needs support before school, we're here to help!

How long does therapy take to show results?

Most parents see noticeable improvement within 3-6 months with regular sessions and home practice. Every child is unique, so we track progress individually.

Can I attend sessions and see what happens?

Absolutely! We encourage parent observation. It helps you understand your child's progress and practice strategies at home. Parent partnership is key to success!

What if my child is shy or hesitant?

That's completely normal! Our warm, play-based environment helps shy children open up naturally. We take it at your child's pace—no pressure, only fun!
